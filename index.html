Negative Spaces

About me: Hi! Im Anna. I am a community manager for Nextdoor, a clinical psychology student at the University of Oregon, a yoga teacher, an educator and a questioner.

This morning I sat drinking my medium roast drip coffee while the rain poured down. I began to wonder why I continue to be drawn to understanding the brain. It almost feels like an innate desire.The same way I love music, nature and a perfect cup of coffee. But why neuroscience? Half way through my coffee, I realized that it is about connection. Learning about the human condition through understanding the brain and the mind adds me to a community of people questioning how we work. It is about this community of questioners.

It made me realize that a core part of my relationship to people, spirituality and religion centers around questions. The validation that we are all in this together allows me to lay my deep existential angst to rest and to focus on the satisfaction of the questions themselves and the simple unexplainable beauty around us. In that same way, I love neuroscience, because I get to be part of a community of people that values the quest, the dissection of our own minds, and discourse through academic articles and musings. I hope to provide a space for my own questions to bubble to the surface as part of the larger community of wonderers.

This blog is called negative spaces. Negative spaces can mean a lot of different things. One of which are questions. Answers are what we all see around us all the time. It is the assumptions we make and the space that is filled to make sense of the world. The negative space is what is between. The moment before we have the relief of an answer or the silence before talking. It can be literal or symbolic and I will explore the spectrum of negative spaces here. By existing in the negative space, I hope to find meaning and purpose, or just sit with the uncomfort or beauty of the space with or without meaning.
